Science of Light

Light And Human
Biology

Light is more than a visual necessity — it's a biological signal that profoundly influences how we feel, function, and live. The human body is naturally tuned to the rhythms of the sun, guided by what's known as the circadian rhythm — our internal clock that regulates sleep, alertness, hormone levels, and overall well-being.

Exposure to the right light at the right time helps maintain this natural cycle, promoting focus during the day and restfulness at night. By mimicking the changing tones of daylight, human-centric lighting helps enhance productivity, mood, and health — making light not just a tool for vision, but a powerful ally for life.

Human Centric Design

The Need Of
Human Centric
Lighting

In today's evolving environments, human-centric lighting plays a vital role in aligning artificial illumination with the natural needs of the human body and mind. It helps improve productivity, reduces eye strain, promotes better sleep cycles, hormonal balance, and overall health. As we spend more time indoors, the importance of lighting that works with us — not just for us — has never been greater.
